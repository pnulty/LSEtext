Allowing super–wealthy migrants to buy their way in to the UK would be a "recipe for disaster", MPs have warned.
Plans to let them bid a minimum of £2.5million for visas at auction or win citizenship in exchange for endowments to good causes simply allows the rich to sidestep immigration controls, said a home affairs select committee report.
Chairman Keith Vaz said "gilts for citizenship" will bring little benefit. "Those who seek to acquire British citizenship should be fit and proper," he added.
