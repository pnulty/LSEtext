Ministers' "overblown rhetoric" about efforts to cut immigration has contributed to a fall in international students reading science, technology, engineering and maths at UK universities, peers have warned.
The Lords Science and Technology Committee said targets to reduce net migration and increase the number of international students were contradictory. The peers also warned some courses relied on the overseas students' fees to be viable.
