The Home Office is investigating claims that migrants who speak no English can buy documents showing that they have passed a supposedly "secure" language test.
The Daily Mail reported that secret filming at one examination centre in east London showed that the certificates — required by anyone wishing to remain in the UK permanently — were being sold for £500. James Brokenshire, the immigration and security minister, said that a full investigation was under way.
