THE number of asylum seekers in Britain will be nearly a third higher this spring than when the Coalition came to power in 2010, a Government minister admitted yesterday.
Tory immigration supremo James Brokenshire said there could be over 23,000 cases in April — a rise of almost 5,400. He blamed the Syria crisis, plus a growing number of bogus claims from immigrants already here.
He said: "We want to offer speedy protection to those who qualify for it. An obstacle for doing so lies in the fact that many claims are unfounded."
