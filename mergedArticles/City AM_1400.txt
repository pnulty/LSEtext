IN BRIEF
UKIP leader Nigel Farage has warned the biggest problem for the Conservatives at the next election is not the increasing popularity of his party, but the authenticity of leader David Cameron. Speaking on the BBC's Andrew Marr show Farage said "voters don't see David Cameron as a Conservative," adding that it is "utterly meaningless" for the Prime Minister to say he has control over immigration while the UK remains a member of the EU.
