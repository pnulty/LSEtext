EU rules are stopping British border guards from trying to weed out illegal immigrants.
Freedom of movement regulations forbid officers from asking EU citizens anything beyond who they are and where they are from, the Government revealed, in answer to criticism of border statistics from MPs.The no-questions rule could mean that non-Europeans using fake or stolen EU passports can slip into the country.
An immigration service source last night said: "It is like working in handcuffs."
