AT last night's lively debate on immigration, hosted by this paper and the City of London, one provocative suggestion from Tottenham MP David Lammy was that perhaps some young Britons are less prepared than immigrants to work. "They don't want to be security guards. They want to watch X Factor," said Mr Lammy. Most young people do want to work but there is a grain of truth in his comment: until all young Britons can match the drive and work ethic of some immigrants, many employers will prefer to employ newcomers. But London needs both to prosper.
